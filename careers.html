Founding Deployment Lead

San Francisco / New York · On-site or hybrid · Full-time or part-time

About Ember

Ember Intelligence builds the back-brain for AI agents inside finance and consulting firms. We are not an agent platform. We are the institutional knowledge layer that plugs into agents firms already run — Claude Code, Cursor, Microsoft Copilot, in-house stacks — and gives those agents structured, navigable access to the firm's full body of work. Spreadsheets, memos, deal books, models, client deliverables, the long tail.

The integration is two files. The complexity is everything underneath them.

About the role

You will be Ember's first Founding Deployment Lead. Your job is to make our design partners successful — to walk into a private-equity firm or a management consultancy, understand how the partners actually do their work, and ship an Ember deployment that materially changes that work.

This is technical work, customer work, and judgment work in roughly equal parts. You will write code most days — Python primarily — but you do not need to be a career engineer. The Ember integration is two files; the work of figuring out which threads to expose, which corpora to index, and how to evaluate retrieval against the firm's real questions is the work, and it is yours. That work requires someone technical enough to ship a custom integration in a week, trusted enough to be in the room when a managing director makes a decision, and personable enough that the partner asks for you by name the next time they call.

You will be one of the first non-engineering technical hires at Ember and the first whose primary loop is the customer rather than the platform. Everything you learn comes back to the product team.

First 90 days

Scoped to full-time. Part-time hires will run the same arc on a proportional timeline.

Weeks 1–3. Deploy Ember at our first three named design partners. Sit in their offices. Map their document corpora. Negotiate the scope of the pilot with their security teams. Ship the first integrations.

Weeks 4–8. Build the evaluation harness — the way Ember measures whether deployments are actually making each firm's agents better. This is half code (test sets, eval pipelines, dashboards) and half judgment (what is the right question to ask?). Whatever you build here becomes the template for every future deployment.

Weeks 9–12. Ship the second cohort of pilots and feed the learnings back to the platform team. By the end of 90 days you should know what Ember needs to build next better than anyone — including the founders.

A typical week

Roughly half your week is customer-facing: working sessions with partners, security reviews with CISOs, deployment work alongside customer engineering teams. Roughly a quarter is technical contribution — shipping the scripts, evals, and integrations a deployment needs. The last quarter is the part most people don't expect: writing. Internal post-mortems, customer case studies, short technical pieces on what's actually working. Your writing will compound.

You are

Technical, but not necessarily a career engineer. You can write Python and SQL well enough to ship things in production. You can read a codebase, debug a retrieval pipeline, and reason about graphs. You do not need a CS degree; you do need to be unintimidated by the actual work of shipping software.

Trusted by the people whose work you would be touching. A managing director at a $40B PE firm will tell you things they would not tell most consultants. They will do it because of how you carry yourself, how you keep promises, and how you handle the moment when something has gone wrong. This is what we mean by trustworthy — not "honest" (table stakes), but capable of being the person in the room people defer to. You may have built this through years at a buy-side firm, a top consultancy, or a forward-deployed role at an applied-AI company. We don't care where; we care that it's real.

Charismatic in the way the room requires. Not stage charisma. Not social-media charisma. You are the kind of person who can sit in a partner meeting, ask the third question nobody else thought to ask, and have the room nod. You can write an email a CISO will read twice. You can be on the trading floor at 7am and feel comfortable there.

Someone who reads documents nobody else wants to read. Half this job is reading IC memos, deal books, client decks, and footnoted Excel models. You should find this interesting — or at least useful — rather than tedious.

Comfortable with ambiguity. The most common state of a deployment lead at a seed-stage company is "we have no idea what to do next, and we have a customer demo on Thursday." If that energizes you, this role is for you. If it doesn't, this role is not.

Strong signals

Any one of these moves you to the top of the pile:

  • You spent two or more years at Palantir, Hebbia, Sierra, Harvey, Decagon, or a similar applied-AI company in a forward-deployed, solutions, or customer-engineering role.
  • You were a PE associate, MBB consultant, or investment-banking analyst who learned to code seriously enough to ship in production.
  • You have built and shipped a non-trivial RAG, retrieval, or knowledge-management system end-to-end — and you can talk about exactly what went wrong with it.
  • You have written publicly about retrieval, knowledge graphs, enterprise AI, or the operational side of finance and consulting in a way that demonstrates real thinking.
  • Part-time path: you are a current associate, senior associate, or principal at a finance or consulting firm who wants to be at the table when this category gets built — and you want to do this as a part-time founding role while you finish your current commitments.

Compensation and logistics

Cash. Your cash compensation is structured as a share of contract revenue you originate and steward — not a fixed salary. We are being direct about this because the right person should see it as the upside it is. At our current stage we are conserving capital, and the right hire will earn substantially more on this structure than on a fixed base. Expected first-year on-target earnings are negotiated against your pipeline ramp and discussed candidly in the offer. Specific split between new-business commission, expansion, and renewal revenue is structured to match full-time or part-time commitment.

Equity. 0.3%–1.0% in stock options, depending on stage of join and full-time versus part-time commitment. Standard four-year vest with a one-year cliff. This range will compress quickly as the company grows; the candidate who joins now gets the early-hire rate.

Commitment — full-time or part-time. Both paths are open. Full-time is the primary path. Part-time works if you are currently inside a finance or consulting firm and can commit roughly 15–25 hours per week to Ember work. Part-time hires get a proportional equity grant and the same revenue-share structure scoped to the accounts they lead. We will not ask part-time hires to choose between Ember and their current firm; we will ask them to be honest about which deployments they can own end-to-end.

Benefits. Full-time hires receive full health, dental, and vision; 401(k); unlimited PTO (we track to make sure people actually take it). Part-time hires receive equity, revenue share, and any benefits coordinated separately depending on their primary employment.

Location. San Francisco or New York preferred. Remote considered for the right person, but expect to be at design-partner sites — typically New York, San Francisco, Boston, and London — for at least one week per month full-time, or one week per quarter part-time. We book the travel.
