About Ember

Why we exist

Agents are only as good as what they can reach.

Every serious firm now runs agents on top of frontier models. Almost none of them have wired those agents into the body of work that actually defines the firm — the memos, the diligence files, the model outputs, the partner-level reasoning that lives across a dozen systems and a thousand documents.

The result is the most expensive form of dilution in software: capable models making mediocre decisions, because the institutional context never reaches them. Ember exists to close that gap. We are the layer between a firm's knowledge and the agents that need it — a citation-aware, permission-aware, multi-tenant graph that any agent stack can call in two files.

How we work

A different kind of company at a different size.

We have built this company differently on purpose. No sales reps. No offshored engineering. No resellers in the middle of any customer relationship. No roadmap that runs ahead of what our design partners are actually using today. That posture costs us reach. It earns us trust.

Every paying customer at Ember is run by one of us — Krishna or Colden, or a founding engineer who built the part of the system the customer is using. Every commit that ends up in front of a fund's data has a name on it. Every retrieval that answers a partner's question has a passage, a chunk, and an edge it can point to. We will be a different kind of company at a different size. Today, we are this one.

— Krishna & Colden, Co-Founders.
