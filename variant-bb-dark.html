✳ The problem

You don’t need another AI pilot.
You need one that sticks.

Most small companies have tried something with AI by now. A chatbot here, a workflow there, a tool someone signed up for and forgot about. Six months later, half of it’s broken, nobody knows what the subscriptions cost, and the thing that was supposed to save time is now something else to maintain.

That’s not a failure of AI. It’s a staffing problem. Tools drift when nobody’s tending them — and a 20-person lab or a 40-person consultancy can’t justify a full-time AI engineer just to keep the fire lit.

That’s the job we do.

No pitch decks. We just come look at how you work.
